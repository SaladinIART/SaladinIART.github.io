# Salbotics.uk Copy Guardrails

Last updated: 2026-05-10

## Core Positioning

Primary line:

> Salbotics Solutions is the public workbench of Muhamad Solehuddin, a Freelance IIoT Consultant helping factories and small businesses turn messy operations into visible, measurable systems.

Short title:

> Freelance IIoT Consultant

Supporting title:

> Industrial Systems Engineer

Availability line:

> Open to full-time roles, contract work, and scoped freelance projects.

## Voice

Use a grounded, proof-led voice:

- factual before persuasive
- specific before broad
- calm before hype
- service-first before product-first
- recruiter-safe before founder-heavy

The site should sound like a serious engineer who can speak to owners, managers, and technical reviewers.

## Approved Language

Use these terms freely:

- Freelance IIoT Consultant
- Industrial Systems Engineer
- practical digital transformation
- industrial systems
- factory visibility
- machine data
- operational dashboards
- scoped sprints
- proof assets
- documented handover
- future roadmap
- open to roles
- contract and freelance work

## Restricted Language

Use only with care and context:

- Founder: acceptable in About/Profile context, but not the hero title.
- Startup: avoid unless discussing Salbotics as a small founder-led practice.
- Platform: acceptable for NEXUS or SOL only when maturity is clear.
- Product: acceptable for future roadmap, not for current SOL availability.
- AI: acceptable only as "AI-assisted" or "AI-augmented" with ownership by the engineer.

## Banned Or Unsafe Claims

Do not say or imply:

- SOL is available to buy today.
- MES-ERP is production-ready.
- NEXUS is a deployed commercial SaaS.
- Salbotics guarantees savings, compliance, tax outcomes, or production improvement.
- Salbotics replaces SAP, Oracle, Dynamics, or licensed consultants today.
- Salbotics provides legal, tax, clinical, or regulated professional advice.
- Client data is used for demos, training, or public proof.
- AI autonomously designed or owns the systems.

## Current Services Wording

Use "sprints" rather than broad transformation promises.

Preferred:

- Factory Energy Visibility Sprint
- SME Ops Digitization Sprint
- Starter Website + Lead Funnel

Use "typical range" for pricing:

- RM 2,500-6,000
- RM 800-2,500
- RM 500-1,500

Avoid:

- "guaranteed ROI"
- "full digital transformation"
- "ERP implementation"
- "compliance-certified"
- "enterprise-grade SaaS"

## Proof Wording

NEXUS:

- Say: "flagship IIoT proof asset", "runnable aluminium decision demo", "architecture and dashboard proof".
- Do not say: "commercial product", "deployed SaaS", "ready for factory rollout".

Factory Energy Monitor:

- Say: "real-world energy monitoring lineage", "factory energy visibility experience", "rebuilt portfolio proof".
- Do not expose confidential client details or claim current live deployment unless separately verified.

SOL / MES-ERP:

- Say: "future roadmap", "planned operations layer", "architecture direction".
- Do not say: "available now", "finished ERP", "MES product for sale".

## Page-Level Rules

Home:

- Lead with Salbotics Solutions and Freelance IIoT Consultant.
- Route visitors to Services, Proof, Freelance Profile, or Contact.
- Mention SOL only as a small future roadmap note.

Services:

- Show only the three locked sprint offers.
- Keep scope, timeline, price range, and handover visible.
- Avoid feature sprawl.

Proof:

- Use proof cards with clear maturity labels.
- Prioritize NEXUS and Factory Energy Monitor.
- Keep technical depth inspectable but not overwhelming.

Freelance Profile:

- Make employment openness explicit.
- Use "open to full-time, contract, and freelance opportunities".
- Do not make the page sound like a pitch for employer-funded Salbotics product work.

Contact:

- Publish email and LinkedIn only.
- Ask visitors to include role, problem, business type, or timeline.
- Do not publish WhatsApp in v1.

## Review Checklist

Before implementation or copy changes ship, check:

- Is the hero recruiter-safe?
- Is the primary CTA contact/hire/interview, not product signup?
- Are the three services clear without implying SOL is available?
- Are NEXUS and Factory Energy Monitor positioned as proof assets?
- Is SOL/MES-ERP labelled future roadmap only?
- Are there any guarantees about savings, compliance, or business outcomes?
- Is the tone grounded and specific?
